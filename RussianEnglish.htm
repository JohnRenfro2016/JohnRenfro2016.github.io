Grammar Terms

Nominative - subject dictionary form "There is [a book(nominative)]."
Accusative - direct object "I see [the book(accusative)]."
dative - indirect object "I'm giving [the book to you( dative )]."
Genitive - possessive "I don't [have the book]."
prepositional - "I'm thinking [about the book( prepositional )]."
Instrumental - "I'm studying [with the book(instrumental)]."

Feminine words - emotions,family members,suffixes -а,-я,-ость,-есть,-зь,-сть,-нь,-ть,-дь,-ль

Masculine words - jobs,days of the week,numbers,geography,suffixes б,г,д,ж,з,к,л,м,н,п,р,с,т,ф,х,ц,ч,ш,щ,-й or -ец -ин -арь

Neutral words - concepts,animals,modern words,diminutive words as in little boy,languages,weather,nature,metals,elements,gerunds(verb to noun),measurements,fruit,suffixes -о,-ко,-е,у,ю

Adjective suffixes -ый,-ой,-ий,-ая,-яя,-ое,-ее

imperfective - have been doing,the verb is done frequently and never over

Passive - is done

Animate - living things

Inanimate - non-living things
